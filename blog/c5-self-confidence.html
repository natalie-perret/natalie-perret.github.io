Self Confidence

In Search Inside Yourself, Chade-Meng Tan considers some definitions of mindfulness and self awareness and concludes that they are one and the same. He goes to further to say that if mindfulness is trainable then self awareness must be trainable too.

Research has shown that simply journalling for two minutes on two consecutive days can bring about immediate improvements in mood. Some helpful prompts for daily journalling practice include: "What I am feeling now is...", "I am aware that...", "What motivates me is...", "I am inspired by...", "Today, I aspire to...", "What hurts me is...", "I wish...", etc.

By deepening his self-awareness, Chade-Meng Tan was able to arrive at a very important insight: we are not our emotions. In order to put this into practice, we need to away from using expressions like "I am angry" and say "I experience anger in my body". This subtle shift allows us to master our emotions.
